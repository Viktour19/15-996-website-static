Class Schedule

All deadlines in this class occur at 8pm ET. Assignment links will become live throughout the class.

Week 1: Introduction, Identity, and Inclusive Discussion

This week students consider the core question: how can identity inform our experience in computer science?

Date Time (ET) Event
Monday 1/31 8pm due: pre-course survey
Thursday 2/3 11:50am – 1:10pm week 1 class [lecture slides]
Friday 2/4 8pm due: post-class reflection


Week 2: Foundations of inequality

This week we consider the core question: why can a policy or standard that aims to treat people equally perpetuate inequality? Students have the option to consider this question through the lenses of (1) implicit bias, (2) intersectionality, and privilege and oppression via (3) cultures and norms and (4) policies.

Date Time (ET) Event
Wednesday 2/9 8pm due: week 2 pre-class activity
Thursday 2/10 11:50am – 1:10pm week 2 class [lecture slides]
Friday 2/11 8pm due: post-class reflection


Week 3: Foundations of identity safety

This week we consider the core question: TODO. Students have the option to consider this question through the lenses of (1) stereotype threat, and (2) interpersonal and (3) structural strategies and for increasing identity safety.

Date Time (ET) Event
Wednesday 2/16 8pm due: week 3 pre-class activity
Thursday 2/17 11:50am – 1:10pm week 3 class [lecture slides]
Friday 2/18 8pm due: post-class reflection


Week 4: Intent versus impact

This week we consider the core question: How should we account for the difference between the intent and impact of our words and actions? Students have the option to consider this question through the lenses of (1) Microaggressions and (2) Equity vs. equality.

Date Time (ET) Event
Wednesday 2/23 8pm week 4 pre-class activity due
Thursday 2/24 11:50am – 1:10pm week 4 class [lecture slides]
Friday 2/25 8pm due: post-class reflection


Week 5: Supporting and advocating for ourselves in the PhD program

This week we consider the core question: What are some strategies and resources for implementing self-care during the Ph.D. program? Students have the option to consider this question through the lenses of (1) Rights and boundaries, (2) Mental health, (3) Self-compassion.

Date Time (ET) Event
Wednesday 3/2 8pm week 5 pre-class activity
Thursday 3/3 11:50–1:10pm week 5 class [lecture slides]
Friday 3/4 8pm due: post-class reflection


Week 6: Allyship and promoting positive change

This week we consider the core question: What can you do to foster inclusion in CSD, or in computer science more broadly? Students have the option to consider this question through the lenses of (1) Allyship and accompliceship, and (2) approaches to making institutional change.
